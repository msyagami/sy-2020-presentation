Old Testament: The Story of Ruth



The story of Ruth plays a big role for the future books of the Bible.


Ruth got married to the sons of Elimelech. But 10 years later, his husband died. Her mother-in-law Naomi, prepared her and her sister-in-law Orpah, to go back to Judah.


Orpah followed her mother-in-law's decision, but Ruth sticked with her.


People we’re shocked that Naomi and Ruth we’re still alive. Naomi said to call her Mara because the Lord made her life "very bitter" and "brought misfortune". (1:20-21 NIV)


Ruth started to glean crops for her mother-in-law and encountered with Boaz. Ruth helped the women of Boaz to glean his crops until Naomi tells Ruth to “rest”.


Ruth moved out, got married to Boaz, and bores a son. Also known as the grandfather of David, the ancestor of Christ. (Luke 3:32)
