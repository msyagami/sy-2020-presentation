Physics & Music: Sound Waves and Musical Instruments



Sound waves, produced by a vibration of a object, transmitting complex waves to the brain, and the brain analyzes the complex waves and convert it to an audible and understandable wave.


Sound waves are usually measured in Hertz (Hz). The higher the Hertz, the higher the compression of waves, the higher the pitch it makes. For example the A440, also known as the A4 in the piano, produces a 440Hz waveform.


Resonance is a phenomenon in which a vibrating system or external force drives another system to oscillate with greater amplitude at specific frequencies. Resonance can cause disasters like the 1940 Tacoma Narrows Bridge, but that doesn't mean it is always dangerous. It can help you play the 5th string in a guitar if you play the 6th string on the 5th fret
