Geometry: Deductive Reasoning

Deductive Reasoning is sometimes important, not just in geometry, but in other subjects too. Maybe not just in school, maybe in life too.

‎‎‎‏‏‎ ‎

Deductive reasoning is different from Inductive reasoning. Inductive just gives a general conclusion, but has a specific observation. Deductive has a specific conclusion, but has a general theory. For example…

‏‏‎ ‎

Students go to school to study; I am studying. So, what does that conclude? I go to school to study.

‏‏‎ ‎

Dogs are kind and loyal to their owner; I have a dog named Loki. Loki is kind and loyal to me.

‏‏‎ ‎

But sometimes it is not useful. Some call it an assumptive reasoning.

‏‏‎ ‎

Monkeys like bananas; I like Bananas. So, am I a monkey? Technically yes, because we came from a different species of monkeys, but no, because I am a human being, not a monkey.
