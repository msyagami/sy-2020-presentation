English: Simple vs. Compound vs. Complex Sentences

A simple sentence, well. It’s just a sentence. It contains a subject and a predicate. It can have compound or multiple subjects or predicates, or even both, as long as it makes sense or expresses one thought. Usually it can contain one clause, therefore develop phrases.

‎‎‎‏‏‎ ‎

“It really does taste great!”

‎‎‎‏‏‎ ‎

“Mondstadt is full of wide plains and rolling hills.”

‎‎‎‏‏‎ ‎

Compound sentences are different, containing independent clauses that can be formed into two different sentences.

‎‎‎‏‏‎ ‎

“The skies here are full of stars, but they aren't the same as the ones back home.”

‎‎‎‏‏‎ ‎

If there are two independent clauses in a sentence, use a semicolon “;” instead.

‎‎‎‏‏‎ ‎

“He got his package today; but I can wait for my package soon.”

‎‎‎‏‏‎ ‎

Complex Sentences on the other hand, contains an independent and a dependent clause.

‎‎‎‏‏‎ ‎

“When he wished for a shining star, Kiana gave her three shining stars.”
